## General

* 3D, 3rd person view, top-down, open-world
* Open World
  * world consists of communities that inhabit a certain area (about 1 km² - 25 km²)
  * settings is placed in Central Europe / Germany
  * communities are connected via long distance traffic connections like Autobahn or Railroads
  * player starts in a smaller community called "Zilleburg" to learn basic mechanics and reach first game goals
  * the player progresses in the same world within the whole game, all changes are persistent
  * the world can be modified by the player
    * Roads can be planned and built (similar to City Skylines)
    * Buildings be planned and built based on prefabs
    * Rooms/floors inside buildings can be assigned to
      * Living quarters/aparments/condos
      * Production facilities
      * Catering/service industry
      * Hideouts
    * Farming/forestry/...
      * Planting/harvesting trees/crops/...

* Zilleburg
  * Rough resemblance of 1940's version of "Radeburg" in Saxony
  * Features railroad, Autobahn, lake, rivers (Röder + Promnitz), Farming and industry
* missions introduce game mechanics
  * Player progression is based on influence, fame, recognition within the current community
  * 
* the player is free to do anything at any point in time
  * Produce: Farming, wood working, tinkering, ...
  * Services: Taxi driving, deliveries, ...
* Unique game mechanic: Post Money Economy
  * Communist-like apporach to economy
  * Basic services and products like food are offered for free but on per-person limits
  * resources are valued based on their utility and scarcity rather than currency
  * Cross-community resource exchange and luxury goods are still traded on currency
  * Wealth is generally shared within the community
  * Still, while generally a good idea, the equality principle principle has it's downsides in motivating people.
    * The player can use "uncompliant" methods in order to gain fame
      * Production, sale, delivery of "uncompliant" goods (weed, alcohol, tobacco, ...)
      * Offering "uncompliant" services (second appartments, prostitution, ...)
      * Procure items generally unavailable to the public (ideas?)
  * Europe was faced by revolts after capitalism 'went too far', increasing the span between poor & rich too wide and workers/farmers could not afford minimal life standards anymore plus capitalism almost destroyed the whole planet due to resource exhaustion and climate change.
* Production Automation
  * In order to reach higher level goals, production of goods needs to be automated
    * Machines
    * Scheduled logistics
    * Delegation to community NPCs
      * NPCs learn and improve in doing jobs
* Game Progression
  * Player starts with a small apartment and access to ordinary jobs
  * By earning fame and recognition due to contributing to the community the player can unlock more advanced jobs and opportunities
  * Higher fame grants access to hideouts and "alternative ways" of earning fame and recognition
  * Higher fame grants access to more advanced production facilities and automation
  * Owning multiple apartments, cars, etc. is considered uncompliant and is not allowed by the community but available by enganging in "uncompliant" activities - thus, hiding them from the community becomes important to keep high fame
  * Owning multiple apartments, cars, etc. might become necessary in order to reach higher level goals
  * Once the highest level in the starting community is reached, the player can move to another community, repeating the same process there with some help from the community of Zilleburg
  * Additional community support is required to reach higher level goals, i.e. securing access to previously unavailable resources like metals, coal, oil, etc. or higher level production facilities like car manufacturing, electronics, chemicals, etc.
  * Alternative ways to gain fame
    * Participate in community events like racing events
* Simulation
  * growing plants, wildlife population, NPCs, production lines, etc.
* Ultimate goals
  * Build a space station in orbit around Earth and colonize the Moon.
	* The player can use the space station to build a new community
  * Build a space elevator on Earth.
  * Reach Mars and Venus, terraform them and build communities there as well.
  * Build a Dyson Sphere around the Sun using resources on Mercury.
  * Use the Dyson Sphere to relocate Mars and Venus towards Earth to reduce interplanetary travelling time.
  * Use the Dyson Sphere to propell the Sun towards the Alpha Centauri system.
  * ...
* ATG is a game that has the potential to be extended with new features over a longer period of time

## Policies
* As the player progresses through the game, he/she can unlock new policies that can be applied to the community
* Policy: Allow or ban alcohol
  * Allowing alcohol changes the fame of the player, but also increases the risk of addiction and crime, thus loss of productivity
  * Banning alcohol decreases the fame of the player but allows the player to run a black market for alcohol, thus increasing the fame of the player
* Policy: Allow or ban weed
* Policy: Allow or ban prostitution
  * Banning prostitution changes the fame of the player, depending on social groups, but allows the player to run a black market for prostitution

## Why the Name: A Terrible Gift?
* A Terrible Gift - the reverse accronym for GTA - tries to introduce an alternative to capitalism
* A Terrible Gift matches very well the games title screen showing Stauffenbergs bomb under the table in the conference room at Wolfsschanze
* With Germany transforming into a Post Money Economy, not everyone is happy with the change, especially those who profited from the old system
* After an initial phase of euphoria, many might find it difficult to find motivation and purpose in a life without money
* ATG: other title options: "Alternative To Greed"

## Missions

* Intro
  * Quick recap of how WW2 ended for Germany
    * June 6, 1944, D-Day: The Allies had successfully landed in Normandy.
    * By July 1944, Operation Bagration had completely destroyed Army Group Centre, one of Germany’s most important Eastern formations.
    * By July 1944, Germany had lost air superiority over both Germany and the front lines.
    * By July 1944, U-boats were still operational but had lost the Battle of the Atlantic by this point.
    * Germany lost the war. But Hitler refused to surrender.
    * July 20, 1944, Stauffenberg coup succeeded and Germanny immediately surrendered

  * WW2 Aftermath
    * Germany was occupied by the Allies
    * Russia took big parts of German territory, as in real history
    * The remaining Allies (USA, UK, France) divided the rest of Germany into zones of occupation
    * The Allies were not interested in punishing Germany, but rather in rebuilding it
    * Stauffenberg and the plotters tried to establish an authoritarian regime, but the Allies opposed this and demanded a democratic government
    * Eventually, Stauffenberg realized the benefits of an democratic approach versus "yet another" authoritarian regime and included more liberal groups to help form a new government
    * Stauffenberg was able to provide three option the German public could vote for 
      * A new democratic government with social capitalism
      * A new democratic government with a post money economy

* Chapter I
  * player wakes up in his/her little and dirty attic apartment at the outskirds of Zilleburg
  * two officers (male + female) show up and ring the bell
  * the two officers came with two different cars
    * one is the official public order office car
    * the second car is a standard sedan
  * the officers give a short introduction to the Post Money Economy setting
  * the officers then explain in a polite way their reason for their visit: The player does not contribute to the community and they wanted to check what is the reason for that.
  * the player then joins one of the officers for a quick tour in order to find a pleasant task to start contributing
    * the player decides which officer to join (male or female)
    * the player gets offered a set of destinations within the community on where to go first
    * the player is offered the driver seat of the second car
    * the chosen officer explains more details, incl. that the sedan is gifted by the community to the player as long as he/she would use it to contribute
    * the chapter ends after visiting a set of destinations that show how the player can contribute and bringing back the officer to the public order office in Zilleburg (center of the town)
      * Offered destinations include: farm, forest, taxi service, delivery service (post office)
