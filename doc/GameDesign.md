## Game Idea

The intent of this game is to create a third-person open-world experience centered around exploration, immersion, and emergent gameplay. The player will embody a customizable character who can interact with a dynamic environment and AI-driven NPCs. The world should feel alive, reactive, and rich with opportunities for discovery.

The game plays in a world where the value of money has been lost, and the player must navigate a society that has adapted to a post-money economy. This setting allows for unique interactions with resources, production, and trade, emphasizing need over wealth. Still attached to old values, the player aims to exploit the system, stretching the new rules while avoiding enforcement in this new world.

The reason for the loss of money is kept vague, allowing players to speculate and create their own narratives. This ambiguity adds depth to the world and encourages players to engage with its mysteries.



## Key Pillars:

Post-Money Economy: The game world operates on a post-money economy where resources are valued based on their utility and scarcity rather than currency. The player explores the benefits of resources, production and trades based on need, rather than wealth. Ironically, the game teases the player into returning to old values, stretching the new rules and avoiding rule enforcement in the new world.

World Permanence & Simulation: The game world is simulated (growing plants, wildlife population, NPCs, production lines, etc.) and changes made by the player are permanent and influence the simulation (destroyed factory buildings do not produce, removed trees stay removed, etc.)

Exploration & Discovery: Encourage players to venture off the beaten path through environmental storytelling, hidden paths, and secrets that reward curiosity.

Immersion & Atmosphere: Focus on a cohesive aesthetic and ambient systems (sound, weather, lighting) that make the world feel authentic and emotionally resonant while still keeping visuals simple.

Agency & Emergence: Allow players to approach situations in multiple ways, using systems that interact (e.g., physics, AI behaviors, crafting, survival mechanics).

Character Progression: Implement growth systems that reward exploration and problem-solving rather than grinding. Allow the player to improve abilities.

Meaningful Interaction: Create depth in world interaction, where even small objects and background characters may serve a purpose or tell a story. Every object serves a purpose and most objects serve multiple purposes.

Farming & Production: To achieve higher goals, the player is encouraged to invest into farming and production lines to maintain or even boost the worlds economic flow.

Crafting & Building: With the help of advanced tools the player can build new or destroy & re-build existing buildings. Crafting is possible at the according production lines.

Transport: Transport is a major feature in the game and serves purpose (transport of goods between production facilities) and entertainment (racing, speeding, drifting)

Reputation: NPCs and the world in general reacts to "good" versus "bad" player actions.

Inspirations:

* Grand Theft Auto 2 for 3rd person top down view, character control, mission design, simplicity, focus on vehicles.
* Satisfactory for production, permanence and transport.
* 7 Days to Die for player progression, permanence of player made changes to the world, farming.
* Breath of the Wild for freedom and systemic design.
* Red Dead Redemption 2 for immersion and environmental detail and reputation system.
* Fallout games for their unique game universe.
* Gothic / ELEX for meaningful NPC behavior and ecosystem dynamics.

Target Platform:

* PC only

Visual Style:

* Stylized realism: grounded proportions with slight exaggeration, clear silhouettes, and a focus on atmospheric detail.

Design Principles:

* Minimize UI; emphasize diegetic feedback.
* Prioritize intuitive controls and meaningful consequences.
* No use of Game Engines like Unreal or Unity.
* Minimize violence
* Base player motivation for progression on things other than money (achievements, scientific progress, in-game romance and family happiness, etc.)
* Allow multiplayer with friends, avoid multiplayer with strangers
* Energy, food, and goods are produced in harmony with natural limits, reflecting a players understanding that in a closed system, balance and symmetry are survival imperatives.
* Procedural quests allow for endless content, but with a focus on quality and variety over quantity.
* Lack of main story avoids linearity and allows players to create their own narratives, keeping them enganged and invested rather than quitting after finishing a linear story.

End Goal:
Deliver a sandbox game world that feels like a place to live in, not just play through. The player should feel a sense of ownership, mystery, and connection to the game world, where every session can unfold in unexpected and memorable ways.
